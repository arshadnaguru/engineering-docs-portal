# What Is VR/XR?

> **Author:** Arshad Naguru · **Last Updated:** March 2026 · **Category:** Explanation

## The Spectrum of Reality

Extended Reality (XR) is an umbrella term covering all immersive technologies:

**Virtual Reality (VR)** — Fully replaces your visual environment with a digital one. You wear a headset and are completely immersed. Used for games, training simulations, architectural walkthroughs, and virtual production previsualization.

**Augmented Reality (AR)** — Overlays digital content onto the real world. Think smartphone apps that place furniture in your room, or smart glasses showing navigation arrows on the street.

**Mixed Reality (MR)** — Digital objects interact with the real world. A virtual ball bounces off your real desk. The boundary between real and digital blurs.

## How VR Works

1. **Display** — Two screens (one per eye) show slightly different images, creating stereoscopic 3D depth
2. **Tracking** — Sensors track your head position and rotation (6 degrees of freedom: x, y, z, pitch, yaw, roll)
3. **Rendering** — A computer or onboard processor renders the 3D scene in real time, adjusting perspective as you move
4. **Input** — Hand controllers, hand tracking, or eye tracking let you interact with the virtual environment

## Frame Rate Matters

VR is demanding because:

- Must render **two views** (one per eye) simultaneously
- Must maintain **90+ fps** — anything lower causes motion sickness
- Latency from head movement to display update must be **under 20ms**

This is why VR development requires powerful GPUs and careful optimization.

## Studio Applications

- **Previsualization** — Directors walk through virtual sets before building physical ones
- **Virtual production** — VR environments displayed on LED walls as camera backgrounds
- **Training** — Safety training, equipment operation in simulated environments
- **Motion capture preview** — See mocap data applied to characters in real time
